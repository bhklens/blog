### Quick summary for the impatient
- **The possession date in the registered agreement is legally binding.** Don’t get misled by expected delivery dates in brochures or verbal commitments by sales people.
- **Get it in writing.** Ensure all promises — amenities, possession dates, specifications — are clearly included in the registered Agreement for Sale. Brochures and verbal assurances don’t hold up.
- **Documentation matters.** The buyers lost part of their claim because they didn’t upload the Agreement and submitted poor-quality brochures.
- **Fit-out possession ≠ real possession.** RERA recognizes possession *only* after OC and essential services are in place.
- **You can claim delayed interest even after taking possession.** Taking the keys doesn’t mean you’ve waived your rights.
- **Delayed interest ≠ penalty.** The buyers' demand for ₹25L for amenities and ₹5L for mental agony was rejected. RERA sees delayed interest as fair compensation, not punishment.

The buyers filed a formal complaint after possession, seeking compensation for:
- Interest on the amount paid for the delayed period.
- Mental agony.
- Non-provision of promised amenities.

The MahaRERA Authority granted interest for the delay, but rejected other compensation claims — offering several important lessons for homebuyers.

### Lessons

#### Agreement for Sale Is the Only Valid Date
The builder argued that the project completion date as extended and mentioned in the MahaRERA Certificate — up to 30.06.2022 — should be treated as the legally binding possession date. To support this, the builder relied on the ruling in Sea Princes Realty v/s. Manoj Votavat & Ors.

However, MahaRERA rejected this argument, stating that the possession date agreed upon in the registered Agreement for Sale prevails over any extensions granted under MahaRERA registration. The Authority relied instead on the Supreme Court's rulings in Newtech Promoters and Imperia Structures, which held that contractual possession dates cannot be overridden by revised RERA timelines, especially when the delay is not attributable to the buyer.

*Always Prioritize the Agreement for Sale Over Brochures or Verbal Assurances*

#### You can claim delayed interest even after taking possession
Under Section 18 of RERA, if you do not withdraw from the project, you’re entitled to monthly interest on the amount paid for the period of delay — even if you eventually take possession.

*You Can File a Complaint Even After Taking Possession*

> *Section 18(1)(a) – RERA Act, 2016*
>
> If the promoter fails to complete or is unable to give possession... he shall be liable on demand to the allottees, in case the allottee does not intend to withdraw from the project, to pay interest for every month of delay, till the handing over of the possession...

#### Fit-Out Possession ≠ Legal Possession
Fit-out possession granted by the developer did not qualify as legal possession under RERA. The Authority treated possession as valid only from the date it was formally offered after obtaining the Occupation Certificate.

> *Para 38*
>
> ...Possession given on 31.12.2019 is stated to be for fit-outs and therefore the same cannot be considered as valid possession in the eyes of law...
